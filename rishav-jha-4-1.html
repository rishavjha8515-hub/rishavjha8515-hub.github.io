Quantum Decoherence in Reissner-Nordström Spacetime: A Multi-Channel Computational Framework

A phenomenological computational framework exploring possible decoherence effects in extreme gravitational fields near charged black holes. Incorporates four independent decoherence channels — thermal (Hawking-corrected), gravitational tidal (Weyl tensor), vacuum fluctuation, and electromagnetic — with charge-dependent corrections derived from surface gravity analysis.

Built entirely in HTML5 and JavaScript without laboratory access. Validated through an empirical study of 27 participants showing a 39.3 percentage point improvement in conceptual understanding (Cohen's d = 1.80, p < 0.001). Accessed 4,700+ times across 30+ countries.

About

The Question
That Found Me

At 12:43 a.m. one night, a question found me: if a black hole swallows everything, where does the information go? I was fifteen. I had no lab, no institutional support, roughly one gigabyte of mobile data per day. I asked it anyway.

I built an interactive computational framework to visualize quantum decoherence near black holes — entirely in HTML5 and JavaScript, without a laboratory. I published it openly on Zenodo, where researchers and students across 30 countries have since accessed it.

When I discovered a counting error in my original participant data, I corrected it publicly — reducing my effect size from 2.67 to 1.80 — because the work has to be real.

I am a first-generation college student from Narsipada, Kandivali East, Mumbai. My father is a labour contractor. My mother is a homemaker. The universe does not care where questions come from.
